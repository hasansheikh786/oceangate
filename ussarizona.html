USS ARIZONA

The USS Arizona (BB-39) was a Pennsylvania-class battleship of the United States Navy, commissioned in 1916. Here's a detailed description:

Construction and Dimensions: The USS Arizona was constructed at the Brooklyn Navy Yard, and it was commissioned on October 17, 1916. It had a length of 608 feet (185 meters) and a beam (width) of 97 feet 1 inch (29.6 meters). Its full load displacement was approximately 31,400 long tons.

Armament: The primary armament of the USS Arizona consisted of twelve 14-inch (356 mm) guns mounted in four triple turrets, two forward and two aft. It also had an array of secondary armaments, including twenty-two 5-inch (127 mm) guns, four 3-inch (76 mm) anti-aircraft guns, and various smaller caliber guns for anti-aircraft defense.

Armor Protection: The ship had armor plating to protect vital areas. This included a main belt armor that was up to 13.5 inches (343 mm) thick, designed to protect against enemy gunfire. Additionally, its armored deck was up to 3 inches (76 mm) thick...



Propulsion: The USS Arizona was powered by four-shaft Parsons steam turbines, which were fed by twelve Babcock & Wilcox boilers. This propulsion system generated a total of 29,000 shaft horsepower, enabling the ship to achieve a top speed of 21 knots (39 km/h).

Service History: The USS Arizona served primarily in the Pacific Fleet during its career. It participated in training exercises, fleet maneuvers, and diplomatic missions in the interwar period. However, it is most famously remembered for its role in World War II.

Attack on Pearl Harbor: On December 7, 1941, the USS Arizona was moored at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, when the Japanese launched a surprise attack on the U.S. Pacific Fleet. During the attack, the ship was hit by several bombs, one of which penetrated the forward magazine, causing a massive explosion. The ship sank rapidly, with the loss of 1,177 crewmen, accounting for almost half of the total casualties suffered by the United States during the attack.

Memorial:The wreck of the USS Arizona still rests at the bottom of Pearl Harbor, and it serves as a memorial to those who lost their lives during the attack. The USS Arizona Memorial, built over the sunken hull, is one of the most visited sites in Hawaii and a poignant reminder of the events of December 7, 1941.

The USS Arizona remains a symbol of American resilience and sacrifice, and its sinking continues to be a significant event in U.S. naval history.
