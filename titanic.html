TITANIC

The RMS Titanic was a British luxury passenger liner that tragically sank on its maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City in April 1912 after striking an iceberg. Here's a detailed description:

Construction and Dimensions:The Titanic was built by the Harland and Wolff shipyard in Belfast, Ireland. It was one of three Olympic-class ocean liners built by the company. The Titanic was approximately 882 feet 9 inches (269 meters) long, with a maximum breadth of 92 feet 6 inches (28 meters) and a height from the waterline to the top of the funnels of 175 feet (53 meters). It was one of the largest and most luxurious ships of its time.

Luxury Amenities:The Titanic was renowned for its opulent interiors and luxurious amenities, designed to cater to the wealthiest passengers. It featured amenities such as a grand staircase, lavish dining rooms, a gymnasium, a swimming pool, a Turkish bath, libraries, and elegant lounges. First-class accommodations were especially lavish, featuring spacious cabins and private promenades.

Passenger Accommodations:The Titanic had accommodations for approximately 2,224 passengers and crew. It offered accommodations across three classes: First, Second, and Third (Steerage). First-class passengers enjoyed the most luxurious amenities and spacious cabins, while Third-class passengers had more modest accommodations...



Engineering:The Titanic was powered by a combination of reciprocating engines and steam turbines, driving three propellers. It was equipped with 29 boilers that powered the ship's engines, generating enough power to propel the massive vessel through the water.

Fateful Voyage:The Titanic departed from Southampton, England, on April 10, 1912, making stops in Cherbourg, France, and Queenstown (now Cobh), Ireland, before setting off across the North Atlantic towards New York City. On the night of April 14, 1912, the ship struck an iceberg and began to sink. Despite efforts to evacuate passengers and launch lifeboats, the ship ultimately foundered and sank in the early hours of April 15, 1912.

Casualties:The sinking of the Titanic resulted in the deaths of over 1,500 people, making it one of the deadliest peacetime maritime disasters in history. The tragedy sparked widespread public outcry and led to significant reforms in maritime safety regulations, including the requirement for ships to carry enough lifeboats for all passengers and crew.

The sinking of the Titanic remains one of the most infamous maritime disasters in history, capturing the imagination of people worldwide and inspiring numerous books, films, and documentaries.
